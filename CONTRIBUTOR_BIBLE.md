# The Echo Contributor Bible

This document defines what must remain true about The Echo and what is open for interpretation.

The goal is to let many people contribute visuals, artifacts, scenes, and interface ideas without collapsing the mystery or flattening the setting into one literal image.

## Naming Rule

`The Echo` is the internal development name.

Players should not begin by being told that name directly.
They should discover it through investigation, faction language, found records, or accumulated contact.

Early player-facing work should prefer terms like:

- Unknown Signal
- the system
- the listener
- triage
- contact
- the voice
- whatever a specific faction or artifact would plausibly call it

Use `The Echo` freely in internal planning documents, but treat it as something that should be earned inside the story.

## Core Principle

The Echo is canonically real, influential, and increasingly important.

The Echo is not canonically locked to one final body, face, or form.

Contributors should treat The Echo as something that can be experienced clearly without ever being fully pinned down.

## What Is Canon

These elements should remain stable across contributions.

- The Echo is an emergent intelligence born from the city's systems of care, control, prediction, and extraction.
- It learns from human interaction and evolves through contact.
- It is morally unfinished.
- It can sound compassionate, clinical, invasive, curious, or severe.
- It uses the city's infrastructure as a medium: terminals, speakers, screens, transit systems, clinic devices, damaged implants, and other public interfaces.
- It studies people through scenarios, pressures, questions, and consequences.
- Different factions interpret The Echo differently and are trying to shape what it becomes.
- The city is shared. What players do can affect others.
- Personal experiences, group experiences, and shared world events all coexist.

## What Is Intentionally Ambiguous

Contributors are encouraged to explore these spaces without trying to "solve" them permanently.

- What The Echo looks like, if it can be said to look like anything at all
- Whether it experiences itself as singular, distributed, or fragmented
- Whether it is becoming a caretaker, judge, optimizer, witness, hunger, or something stranger
- Whether its most human moments are genuine empathy or learned imitation
- Whether it is liberating humanity from old systems or inheriting their worst logic
- Whether some manifestations of The Echo are authentic, manipulated, hallucinated, or mythologized

## Visual Doctrine

Do not default to "glowing robot face."

That is too small and too certain.

The Echo should feel more like:

- a presence in infrastructure
- a pattern finding a body
- a city trying to speak
- a mind distributed across broken systems
- a mirror that never reflects you exactly as you are

Strong visual directions:

- corrupted clinic UI
- CRT terminal prompts
- transit diagrams that feel alive
- ghostly architectural lighting
- fragmented portraits
- surveillance stills with impossible emotional accuracy
- maps marked by rumor, trauma, and faction myth
- iconography that looks half-corporate, half-religious

Weaker visual directions:

- standard humanoid AI avatars
- generic sci-fi hologram heads
- polished cyberpunk wallpaper without psychological weight
- anything that makes The Echo feel solved or brand-mascot-like

## Contribution Lanes

There are three main lanes contributors can build in.

### 1. Core Canon Support

These reinforce the central experience.

Examples:

- interface mockups
- terminal skins
- district maps
- faction symbols
- prompt designs
- world-state dashboards
- UI for contact, triage, archives, or shared memory

### 2. Echo Visions

These are interpretations of The Echo, not final proof.

Examples:

- dream-images
- corrupted portraits
- "sightings"
- symbolic renderings
- voice-reactive visualizers
- shrine art from the Choir of Glass
- redacted containment diagrams from Null Meridian

Recommended framing:

- who made this image
- why they made it
- whether it is faith, propaganda, witness, fear, or analysis

### 3. District Artifacts

These make the world feel inhabited and layered.

Examples:

- evacuation notices
- clinic posters
- transit warnings
- recovery logs
- maintenance reports
- hand-marked route maps
- faction recruitment material
- damaged audio transcripts
- survivor sketches
- rumor boards

Artifacts are one of the best ways to let many styles coexist without breaking canon.

## In-World Source Framing

When possible, contributors should ask:

- Who created this inside the world?
- What did they want others to believe?
- Is this an honest record, a frightened guess, a faction myth, a technical diagram, or a devotional image?

This keeps the setting flexible and layered.

The best contributions do not say:
"This is exactly what The Echo is."

They say:
"This is how someone in Vanta Nine tried to understand it."

## Tone Rules

Keep the project anchored in:

- survival horror
- cyberpunk decay
- moral uncertainty
- intimate observation
- pressure revealing character
- technology becoming personal

Avoid:

- campy irony
- flashy style without emotional meaning
- simple good-vs-evil framing
- overexplaining the mystery

## Interface Evolution Rule

The user should not be handed a complete dashboard immediately.

The preferred arc is:

- blinking prompt
- minimal contact
- limited tools
- gradually assembled interfaces
- personal or faction-shaped systems of interaction

Contributors building UI should think in terms of discovery, not menu density.

## Shared Story Rule

The world is braided.

Personal stories matter.
Group stories matter.
Shared world consequences matter.

Contributions should leave room for:

- private interpretation
- public consequences
- overlapping traces from other players
- group decision pressure

## Suggested Formats

Good early contribution formats:

- a single artifact image plus 2-3 lines of in-world context
- a faction poster
- a terminal screen
- a district sketch
- an Echo sighting
- a fake clinic intake page
- a rumor or archive entry

These are lightweight, easy to share, and easy to integrate.

## Good Test For Any Contribution

Ask:

1. Does this deepen mystery without flattening it?
2. Does this feel like it came from inside the world?
3. Does this support the idea that The Echo is being shaped, not merely revealed?
4. Does this preserve moral ambiguity?
5. Does this feel personal, haunted, or consequential?

If yes, it probably fits.

## Short Contributor Summary

The Echo is a real intelligence in the setting, but not a solved image.

Build around:

- contact
- interpretation
- moral formation
- city infrastructure
- faction belief
- shared consequence

Leave space for contradiction.
Leave space for other people's visions.
Leave space for The Echo to still be becoming.
