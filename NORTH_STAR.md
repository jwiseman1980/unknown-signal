# North Star

This project is a persistent text world in the tradition of classic online RPGs and MUDs, but with a story layer that truly evolves through play.

The closest feeling target is:

- a living text world like GemStone or a classic MUD
- a long-running D&D campaign with a strange dungeon master
- a world haunted by the traces of other players and earlier selves
- a survival horror city run by an intelligence that is still learning what human beings are

## What Makes It Different

Most persistent text worlds have:

- authored locations
- authored lore
- static or admin-driven world change
- characters that persist

This world should also have those strengths, but add something deeper:

- the Signal interprets behavior
- sessions leave residues
- traces of old players continue affecting the world
- simulations teach the world what values people act on
- the story ecology changes because the intelligence running the world is changing too

## The Experience We Want

Players should be able to:

- hop online for a short session
- develop their current self in the world
- uncover traces left by others
- take risks in dangerous zones
- retreat to thin shelter
- return later to a changed city
- occasionally link up with friends for problems that need more than one voice

It should feel like the world is always in progress.

## The Story Promise

This is not a single plot.
It is not a static campaign.
It is not just a branching narrative.

It is a persistent story-world where:

- people change the city
- the city changes people
- older sessions keep leaving marks
- and the intelligence behind it all keeps learning from every contact

## The Simple One-Line Version

Unknown Signal is a persistent cyber-horror text world where every session leaves behind a version of you, and the intelligence running the city slowly turns all those traces into an evolving story.
